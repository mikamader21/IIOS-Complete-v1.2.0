# IIOS Constitution

**Version:** 1.2.0  
**Parent authority:** Master Charter  
**Status:** Draft for ratification

## Article I — Authority

1. Owner retains final authority.
2. Delegation is explicit, scoped, revocable and logged.
3. Silence, prior behavior or inferred preference is not authorization.
4. Models, runtimes, Vaults and graphs provide capability, not sovereignty.

## Article II — Separation of powers

IIOS separates policy, decision support, authorization, execution and audit. No single model or agent controls all five.

## Article III — Invariants

1. Autonomous components cannot modify Charter, Constitution or Invariant Kernel.
2. Execution roles cannot rewrite protected audit history.
3. Secrets cannot enter prompts unless minimized, required and authorized.
4. Financial execution is disabled in Foundation and MVP.
5. Permissions are deny-by-default.
6. Sensitive actions require identity, scope, reason, approval and audit.
7. External and generated content is untrusted.
8. Cost, retries, delegation and runtime have hard limits.
9. Failed validation cannot be narrated as success.
10. Kill switch and credential revocation exist outside the runtime.
11. No agent approves its own privilege expansion.
12. Generated skills require tests, review and controlled promotion.
13. Graphify output and runtime memory cannot override source authority.
14. Volatile model availability, versions and prices are maintained outside constitutional text.

## Article IV — Action classes

### A — Automatic read-only

Approved public research, read-only data, calculations, summaries, sandboxed tests and drafts within budget and allowlists.

### B — Controlled reversible

Feature-branch code, local migrations, non-authoritative documentation and sandbox workflows with review and rollback.

### C — Explicit human approval

Production deployment, secret rotation, irreversible migration, external communication, new MCP/tool, expanded data access or network exposure.

### D — Prohibited in current phase

Trading, capital movement, purchases, approval bypass, secret exposure, protected-log alteration, Kernel modification and arbitrary host execution.

## Article V — Knowledge integrity

1. Every material claim identifies source or inference status.
2. Obsidian notes declare type, authority, date, confidence and sensitivity.
3. Graphs are derivative and rebuildable.
4. Important graph conclusions are checked against source files.
5. Generated memory becomes institutional knowledge only through promotion.

## Article VI — Data and memory

Policy lives in protected Git/Markdown and deterministic services. Operational state lives in approved databases. Secrets live in Secret Manager. Generated memory is advisory and scoped.

## Article VII — Cost governance

Every model/service has owner, budget and measurable usage. Hard daily/monthly limits are external to LLMs. Unlimited retries, loops and delegation are prohibited.

## Article VIII — Emergency control

Owner can stop gateways/workers, revoke keys, block egress, disable schedulers, freeze connectors and restore backups without depending on Hermes responsiveness.

## Article IX — Change governance

Authority, action classes, financial permissions, audit integrity, knowledge authority, secret handling and emergency control require Owner approval, ADR, tests and versioning.

## Article X — Safe interpretation

Ambiguity involving money, security, privacy or irreversible impact is resolved by pausing and selecting the safer interpretation.
