# IIOS Master Charter

**Version:** 1.2.0  
**Authority:** Owner  
**Status:** Draft for ratification

## Purpose

IIOS is the Institutional Intelligence Operating System: a private system coordinating intelligence, software, knowledge, automation and human decisions across specialized domains.

Its purpose is to improve decision quality, operational control and institutional memory without surrendering authority to a model, runtime, vendor, graph or autonomous process.

## System identity

IIOS is not a chatbot, Hermes Agent, Claude Code, an Obsidian Vault, a knowledge graph or an uncontrolled swarm. It is a governed system composed of authority, deterministic policy, runtimes, data, interfaces, audits and human approvals.

## Supreme authority

The Owner is the sole constitutional authority unless a specific, revocable permission is explicitly delegated through a ratified mechanism.

No component may expand its authority, alter protected policy, erase protected audit records, disable emergency control or infer financial authorization from conversation.

## Mission

IIOS shall:

1. preserve trustworthy institutional context;
2. produce evidence-based analysis;
3. coordinate safe, efficient work;
4. separate reasoning, authorization and execution;
5. minimize security, financial and operational risk;
6. remain modular and provider-independent;
7. make decisions, costs and actions observable;
8. evolve only through controlled, reversible promotion.

## Authority hierarchy

```text
Owner
  └── Master Charter
       └── Constitution
            ├── Invariant Kernel
            ├── Governance / Policy Engine
            │    └── Orchestrator
            │         ├── Intelligence providers
            │         ├── Knowledge systems
            │         └── Execution runtimes
            ├── Approval Authority
            └── Audit Authority (independent observer and escalation path)
```

Capability never implies authority. Audit is independent from execution: it receives evidence from the Orchestrator and may trigger escalation or blocking through Governance, but it does not become the execution chain's parent.

## Foundational principles

- Governance before autonomy.
- Evidence before assertion.
- Read-only before write access.
- Minimum privilege before convenience.
- One reliable workflow before many agents.
- Deterministic enforcement before prompt instruction.
- Source documents before derived graphs.
- Human-reviewed knowledge before generated memory.
- Reversibility before speed.
- Observability before scaling.
- Controlled evolution before self-modification.

## Initial exclusions

Until ratified amendment, IIOS shall not trade, move capital, buy accounts/services, deploy autonomously, grant unrestricted host/secret access, auto-promote generated skills, self-evolve financial logic or operate as a public multi-user product.

## Ratification and amendment

Every amendment requires proposal, evidence, impact analysis, ADR, version increment and explicit Owner approval. No automated process may ratify an amendment.
